More than your average career fair

xFair is more than booths, name tags, and blazers - it’s an exhibition of the latest pursuits by companies and students combined. The result is a unique showcase of raw talent, raw ideas, and raw passion. Previously, with 2000+ MIT undergraduate and graduate students in attendance, xFair is MIT’s largest student-run career fair and tech expo.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is xFair?

We have merged TechX's TechFair and TBP's Career Fair to present xFair: an interactive career fair with a focus on company demos and our very own MIT student projects.

What distinguishes xFair from traditional career fairs?

Many companies at xFair will be displaying cool demos, so you can check out the products that you'll help build at your future job. In addition to company demos, we'll also be displaying cool projects made by students, so come to support your friends' and classmates' projects!

Should I bring my resume?

Yes! Also be sure to drop your resume online when the link opens so employers can find you before the fair.

How is xFair held this year?

Due to the circumstances caused by Covid-19, we will be holding xFair completely virtually this March on Brazen. To reflect this new format, we have made necessary changes to our sponsorship tiers and related events. However, as always, our top priority is still to give the best possible experience for both employers and students.

What's the dress code?

Smart casual.

Where can I get more information?

Student information will be updated closer to the event. You can also follow MIT xFair on Facebook or Twitter to stay updated!
